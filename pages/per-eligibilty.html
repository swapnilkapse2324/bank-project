Punjab personal Loan : Eligibility

1. Credit score

  • Applicants with credit scores of 750 and above are considered to be more financially disciplined and hence, are less likely to default.

    • 2.Employment Type and Employer Reputation

  • Among salaried and self-employed applicants, lenders offer personal loans to salaried applicants at lower interest rates due to their higher income certainty.

    • 3. Age

  • Most banks and NBFCs offer personal loans to applicants in the age group of 18 years to 65 years./li>

    4. Minimum Income

  • Higher income indicates higher capacity to repay your loan on time, which further implies lower risk for lenders.

    • 5.Work/Business Experience

  • Many lenders specify that salaried individuals need to have a minimum total work experience of 1 year, with others requiring longer work experience to be eligible for availing personal loans.

    • 6. Repayment Capacity

  • Banks and NBFCs usually sanction personal loans to applicants whose EMI/NMI ratio is not more than 50% to 55%, which may vary across lenders.
